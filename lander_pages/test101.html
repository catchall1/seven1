Facts About Ivermectin

Ivermectin is a World Health Organization (WHO) essential medicine and it is approved by both the WHO and FDA as an antiparasitic drug for humans. Studies have indicated that this medicine has antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties leading to an increasing list of therapeutic indications.

Although the US Food and Drug Administration (FDA) has not approved ivermectin to treat COVID-19, it is not illegal or unreasonable for the appropriate licensed healthcare prescribers to recommend ivermectin “off-label” for the treatment and prophylaxis of COVID-19. Off-label prescribing is not something that the FDA even has the authority to outlaw because the FDA exists to regulate drug approval not drug prescribing. Prescribing a drug to a patient for an unapproved use is entirely legal if the prescriber deems it appropriate and beneficial for the patient.

Off-label prescribing is a very common practice and is especially prudent when there is no FDA-approved drug available to treat a person’s condition, or when the prescriber has reviewed evidence that a certain drug works well for a particular off-label use. The prescribers at issue have reviewed substantial recently emerging evidence strongly indicating that using ivermectin for treatment and prophylaxis of COVID-19 results in favorable outcomes and lower mortality rates. It is important to note that the pharmacy is not promoting the use of ivermectin as it relates to treatment and prophylaxis of COVID-19. 

 The pharmacy is also not advocating self-medicating, large doses, or use of medicines intended for animal use. Any dispensing of ivermectin by the pharmacy is upon a patient-specific valid prescription. The prescribers are recommending ivermectin in their own professional judgment and are writing prescriptions for a medicine intended for human use at low dosages after taking into account the patient’s body size, other medications, and allergies.

It is not unusual for prescribers to repurpose medicines, especially when, as is the case with ivermectin, the medicine has a known well-established safety profile after decades of use and rare adverse side effects at usual doses.

Interestingly, the FDA cautions against ivermectin use to treat COVID-19 while at the same time acknowledging off-label use stating the following in one of its consumer education pages:

Has your healthcare provider ever talked to you about using an FDA-approved drug for an unapproved use (sometimes called an “off-label” use) to treat your disease or medical condition?

If your health care provider writes you an ivermectin prescription, fill it through a legitimate source such as a pharmacy, and take it exactly as prescribed.
